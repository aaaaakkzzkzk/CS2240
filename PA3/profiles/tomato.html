Tomato Frog

Dyscophus Antongilii

Tomato frogs are any of the three species of genus Dyscophus (family Microhylidae): D. antongilii, D. insularis, or D. guineti.[1] Dyscophus is the only genus in subfamily Dyscophinae. They are endemic to Madagascar. Red and plump, the tomato frog is very much like a big, ripe tomato. The brightest and largest individuals of the species are the females. The tomato frog is native to the tropical rainforests of northeastern Madagascar, specifically the area of Antongil Bay.
